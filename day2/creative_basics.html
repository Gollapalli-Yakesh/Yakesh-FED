Che Guevara: Revolutionary Icon

Early Life

Ernesto Rafael Guevara de la Serna, better known as Che Guevara, was a key figure in the Cuban Revolution and remains one of the most iconic and controversial revolutionary leaders in modern history.

Childhood

  • Full Name: Ernesto Rafael Guevara de la Serna
  • Birthdate: June 14, 1928
  • Birthplace: Rosario, Argentina

Che Guevara was born into a middle-class family in Argentina. His early years were marked by an interest in literature and a curiosity about the socio-political conditions of Latin America.

Education

Che Guevara pursued a medical degree at the University of Buenos Aires, where he developed an interest in social issues and Latin American politics.

Revolutionary Activities

Cuban Revolution

In 1955, Guevara met Fidel Castro and joined the July 26 Movement, which aimed to overthrow the U.S.-backed dictator Fulgencio Batista in Cuba.

  • Role: Commander in the Cuban Revolutionary Army
  • Outcome: Overthrew Batista’s regime in 1959 and established a socialist government in Cuba
